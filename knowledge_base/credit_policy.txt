# TechVest Internal Credit Policy

## Section 1: Debt-to-Income (DTI) Regulations
[Clause CP-DTI-01]
The maximum allowable Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio is 45% for salaried applicants and 40% for self-employed applicants. DTI is calculated as: (Total Existing EMIs + Proposed Loan EMI) / Gross Monthly Income. Any application with a DTI exceeding these thresholds must be flagged as REFER or DECLINE depending on credit history.

[Clause CP-DTI-02]
For borderline applicants (DTI between 40% and 45% for salaried), the application may be APPROVED only if the credit score is above 750 and the employment stability is greater than 3 years. Otherwise, it must be referred.

## Section 2: Credit Score Thresholds
[Clause CP-CS-01]
Standard credit assessment categories based on Bureau Credit Scores:
- Credit Score >= 750: Eligible for automatic recommendation of APPROVE, subject to satisfactory DTI and documentation.
- Credit Score 650 to 749: Standard category, must be flagged as REFER for manual underwriter review.
- Credit Score < 650: Low category, must be automatically flagged as DECLINE.

[Clause CP-CS-02]
No loan application can be approved if there is any active default or write-off reported in the last 12 months, regardless of the credit score.

## Section 3: Income stability rules
[Clause CP-INC-01]
The minimum monthly income required for a loan application is:
- Salaried applicants: INR 25,000 per month.
- Self-employed applicants: INR 35,000 per month.
Gross monthly income must be verified using the last 3 months' salary slips and 6 months' bank statements.

[Clause CP-INC-02]
Applicants must show a continuous employment history of at least 12 months with their current employer, or at least 24 months of active business operations if self-employed.
